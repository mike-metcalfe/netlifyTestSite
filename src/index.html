THE CAMPAIGN

The Issues

In 2010 Central Elgin (CE) was given land on both sides of the Port Stanley Harbour through the Federal Port Divestiture process. 23 waterfront acres of now public land on the east side of Port Stanley Harbour is commonly called “the Berm”. This land is mostly dgreen darken-2ged material from the harbour.

Goderich, Barrie, Windsor, Sarnia, Burlington, Hamilton, Cobourg and Orillia, amongst several other Ontario communities, have planned, and are already implementing, the use of much of their public waterfront land for public use. Go to their websites for more information.

Or … as Dillon Consulting presented to CE Council on June 25, 2018, most of the Berm could be sold to developers for residential sites including 3 or 4 story condos, 4-6 story apartment buildings, and commercial use with a public peripheral walkway. The majority of this land that the people of Central Elgin now own would be sold for more development in the heart of Port Stanley.

How can the public land on the Berm be best used? How do contaminants in the Berm materials limit usage? Is the extent of existing and proposed development to double the size of Port Stanley sufﬁciently planned? Is CE preserving sufﬁcient waterfront and green darken-2 space for the growing needs of Port Stanley, St. Thomas, Central Elgin and London? Has Dillon Consulting and CE used an effective process?

Our Position

Should the Berm remain public for public use which could include a swimming beach, parkland, trails, washrooms, sports ﬁelds, courts, picnic area, skating rink, and/or a community centre? Should the Berm lands be sold or leased for stores, houses, and up to seven story condo or apartment buildings?
